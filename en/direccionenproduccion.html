Service 03

Art Direction in Production

The project is not only designed. It is directed through execution.

This service integrates design, visual direction and production support to ensure the visual world stays coherent through installation, prelight and filming.

Start Your Project

What it is and what it ensures during execution

A full-scope service for projects that need art direction during execution, not only during the design phase.

Here, visual direction does not stay inside a document. It is sustained through real production decisions, purchasing, installation, on-site adjustments and final visual validation.

It ensures the project keeps visual coherence from definition to execution, so the design does not get diluted once production begins.

Medium-scale productions

•

Series

•

•

•

Projects where the design needs to be actively sustained during production

•

Teams that need more than a visual proposal and require real direction on site

What it solves

  • Sustains the visual criteria through purchasing, installation and filming
  • Supports real-time art direction decisions without losing coherence
  • Aligns design, production and execution under one visual direction
  • Corrects deviations before they affect the final result
  • Prevents the project from being executed by inertia without real visual control

Included phases

Phase 0

Strategic project reading

The visual objective is aligned with real production scope, timing and execution priorities.

Phase 1

Foundational visual direction

A guiding criterion is defined to direct art, materiality and staging decisions throughout the process.

Phase 2

Concept development

The project narrative is translated into a visual language that can be applied across each operational front.

Phase 3

Spatial / set design application

The design is adjusted to physical, technical and real-use conditions to prevent gaps during installation and filming.

Phase 4

Visual system and materiality

Palettes, finishes, textures and specifications are defined to maintain coherence across suppliers and purchasing.

Phase 5

Preparation for execution

Pre-installation decisions are structured so the team can execute with clarity and without improvisation.

Phase 6

Purchasing supervision

Materials and purchased resources are validated to maintain the defined visual standard.

Phase 7

Installation direction

The on-site implementation is directed and deviations are corrected in real time.

Phase 8

Prelight and visual validation

Composition, readability and continuity are checked before filming or opening.

Phase 9

Continuity and final visual control during execution

Final execution is validated so it stays coherent with the direction defined across production.

Pricing structure

This service is divided into three parts:

Direction fee

My art direction work and decision-making throughout the process.

Team fees

Assistant, supervisor or operational support, depending on the scope of the project.

Production budget

Purchases, materials, suppliers, rentals and execution costs, paid directly by the client.

This service is not billed as a percentage.

What’s not included

  • Production budget
  • Supplier payments
  • Additional team fees
  • Installation resources not covered by the client
  • Scope changes beyond the approved plan

This service directs execution, but it does not absorb production costs or replace the operational structure the project needs in order to be carried out properly.

Requirements

Art assistant or operational support

Minimum installation crew depending on the project

Defined or viable production budget

Approved schedule before execution begins

This service does not work well if full art direction is expected without a team, without structure or without real execution capacity.

Limits and revisions

Scope is defined according to budget and project complexity

Changes outside the approved phase affect timing and cost

Adjustments after approval require reevaluation

The service is structured around the real production schedule

Result

A project that is both designed and executed under coherent visual direction, with real control over the decisions that shape the final outcome.

This service is right for you if...

Good fit if

  • the project is already moving into execution or about to enter that stage
  • having a design is not enough and it needs to be sustained during production
  • you need real art direction during purchasing, installation and filming
  • you want to prevent the final result from losing strength compared to the original concept

Not the right fit if

  • you still need strategic clarity only
  • the project is not yet in production
  • you are only looking for a visual proposal without execution support
  • there is no minimum team or realistic structure in place to sustain the process

What makes this different from Art Direction & Production Design

In the previous service, the project is fully designed and prepared for execution. Here, in addition to being designed, execution is actively directed.

That means accompanying real production decisions, sustaining the visual criteria on site and validating that what gets built remains coherent with what was originally defined.

When the project requires real visual direction during execution, this is the right level of service.

If design alone is not enough and the project needs direction across purchasing, installation, team coordination and visual continuity, this is the service that carries it through to materialization.
